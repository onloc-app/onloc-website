you have to log in to the instance of your choice. Enter the server's URL and your credentials into their corresponding fields.

## Permissions

Multiple permissions are required by the app to provide most of the features
supported by Onloc. These permissions have been grouped by features so you can
enable only those you need. Start by dragging the handlebar at the bottom of
your screen to open the services and permissions sheet.

<ThemedImage
  alt="Permissions screen"
  sources={{
    light: "/img/android/light/permissions.png",
    dark: "/img/android/dark/permissions.png",
  }}
  style={{ maxWidth: "400px" }}
/>

Here, you can grant permissions as you see fit.

## Enabling Location Service

### Selecting a Device

In order to enable the location service which sends data periodically to your server,
you first need to select a device to which your location data will be attached.
Tap on "Select a device" at the top of your screen. The devices you created should
appear, allowing you to select one.

<ThemedImage
  alt="Device selection screen"
  sources={{
    light: "/img/android/light/device_select.png",
    dark: "/img/android/dark/device_select.png",
  }}
  style={{ maxWidth: "400px" }}
/>

### Update Frequency

From the main screen, drag the handlebar at the bottom to open the services and
permissions sheet. The interval picker lets you choose a frequency at which your
location will be sent to the server.

A shorter interval will provide you with more accurate data, but may drain your
device's battery faster, while a longer interval will update less often,
leaving you with less data, but costing less battery life.

:::tip
We recommend setting the interval to 15 minutes, giving you up-to-date data while
costing little battery life.
:::
